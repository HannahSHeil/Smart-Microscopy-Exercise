Dataset: HeLa Cells with Bacterial Infection (Widefield Microscopy)

Description:
This dataset contains multi-channel microscopy images of HeLa cells infected with Yersinia pseudotuberculosis bacteria. The images were acquired as large field-of-view overview scans with 3x3 fields, stiched to a single image. One dataset is downsized by 2x2 binning (Median).


Channels:
- C1: DIC (transmitted light, cell morphology)
- C2: AF647 (Spy650–fastAct, actin cytoskeleton)
- C3: GFP (bacteria signal)

Microscope Setup:
- Microscope: Nikon Ti2-E inverted wide-field fluorescence microscope in a BSL2 environment
- Objective: Nikon Plan Apo λ 10×, NA 0.45
- Focus stabilization: Perfect Focus System (PFS)

Camera:
- Nikon DS-Qi2 CMOS camera

Optical Configuration:
- GFP (FITC filter):
  Excitation 457–487 nm, Emission 503–538 nm
- AF647 (Cy5 filter):
  Excitation 590–645 nm, Emission 659–736 nm

DIC imaging used transmitted light optics.

Environmental Conditions:
- Temperature: 37 °C
- CO₂: 5%
- Humidified atmosphere
- Environmental chamber: Okolab

Cell Culture Conditions:
- HeLa cells cultured in FluoroBrite medium
- Supplemented with Fetal Calf Serum (FCS)
- Supplemented with GlutaMAX

Usage:
This dataset is suitable for:
- Cell segmentation (e.g. CellSAM)
- Detection of bacterial infection (GFP channel)
- Spatial analysis of host–pathogen interactions
- Subpopulation selection
- Simulation of adaptive microscopy workflows
